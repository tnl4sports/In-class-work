Green Poem Bean

Green Beans In tidy rows of garden green,
They hang like jewels with subtle sheen—
A crisp, clean snap, a summer sound,
A treasure pulled from fertile ground.
They steam and sizzle, gently tossed,
In butter pools where flavors cross;
Or chilled in salads, bright and fresh,
A crunchy bite in every mesh.
Humble pods with quiet grace,
No need for glory, show, or chase—
Yet on the plate they always seem
To bring a taste of earth and dream.
